Analysis of data

Data entry automation Manizales Cathedral

The first step in developing the system was to identify the types of occupation that would be registered: ossuaries, rental houses, and parking lots. With this information, the structure of the Excel worksheets was planned, ensuring that each sheet had specific and relevant columns for each type of occupation. This planning included the definition of fields such as tenant name, start and end date of the contract, rental value, payment status, and observations. Clear organization of the data was crucial to facilitate the development of efficient macros and ensure the integrity of the stored information.

To simplify the registration process, input forms were developed using UserForms in VBA (Visual Basic for Applications). Each form was designed to capture data specific to each type of occupation. The forms include mandatory fields to prevent essential information from being omitted, and when you complete the data, the form automatically stores it in the corresponding sheet. This not only speeds up the recording of information, but also reduces the likelihood of human error.

To avoid duplication and optimize resource management, a macro was implemented that checks availability before each new registration. This macro checks the worksheets and alerts the user if the resource is already busy or if there are any inconsistencies in the data. This guarantees that each resource (ossuary, house or parking lot) is correctly assigned and prevents errors when making new registrations.

Macros were developed that monitor the expiration dates of each contract and send alerts when they approach. These alerts are generated automatically and are designed so that responsible personnel receive an on-screen notice whenever a contract is about to expire or a payment is pending. In this way, you can manage the tracking of renewals and avoid loss of income due to lack of tracking.

Finally, data protection was configured on the worksheets and macros to ensure that only authorized personnel can modify the records. Passwords were applied to worksheets to restrict access and editing options were disabled on input forms. This ensures that information is secure and that sensitive data is not altered without authorization.

To ensure the correct functioning of the system, multiple tests were carried out on each of the functions developed. These tests included validation of data entry forms, verification of automatic reports, and effectiveness of expiration alerts. Additionally, the availability control macro was tested to confirm that it detected any duplicate occupancy. During this process, areas for improvement in the speed of execution and clarity of the alert messages were identified, allowing for optimization of the VBA code and adjustments to the forms. These improvements resulted in a smoother, easier-to-use system, meeting automation goals and significantly reducing manual administration time. The final system offers a reliable and optimized process for resource management, meeting the security and precision requirements required by the Manizales Cathedral.
